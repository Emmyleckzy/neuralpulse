Pi Network in 2026: Is It Finally Worth Something?

📅 Jun 4, 2026⏱ 7 min read✍️ NeuralPulse Staff

Pi Network has been one of the most debated projects in crypto for years. Millions of people mined it on their phones, waited through delay after delay, and wondered if it would ever amount to anything. Now that Pi is in Open Mainnet, the question everyone's asking is: was it worth it?

🔵 Quick background: Pi Network launched its Open Mainnet in early 2025, allowing users to finally transfer and trade Pi coins. It is now listed on several exchanges including OKX, Gate.io, and Bitget.

What Is Pi Actually Trading At?

Pi's price has been volatile since mainnet launch, which is typical for newly listed assets. The coin has traded anywhere from $0.40 to $2.80 in the months since listing, driven largely by speculation and the massive supply unlocking from long-term miners.

The key factor affecting price is the lockup system. Users who locked their Pi for longer periods are receiving higher mining bonuses — and their coins are being released gradually, which has helped prevent a complete price collapse from the massive available supply.

How to Cash Out Your Pi

If you have Pi in your wallet and want to sell, here's the process:

  • Complete KYC verification in the Pi Network app if you haven't already
  • Migrate your Pi from the app wallet to the Pi Mainnet blockchain
  • Create an account on an exchange that lists PI (Gate.io and OKX are most liquid)
  • Send your Pi to the exchange deposit address
  • Trade PI for USDT or another stablecoin, then withdraw

⚠️ Warning: Be extremely careful of scam platforms claiming to let you sell Pi. Only use verified, reputable exchanges. Many fake Pi trading sites have stolen funds from users.

Is It Too Late to Benefit?

This depends entirely on how much Pi you have and what you mined it at. Early miners who accumulated tens of thousands of Pi are sitting on significant value even at current prices. Late miners with a few hundred Pi have much smaller returns.

The bigger question is where Pi goes from here. The project has over 60 million KYC-verified users — a genuinely impressive user base. If the Pi ecosystem (apps, marketplace, payments) develops real utility, the coin could appreciate further. If it remains primarily a speculative asset with no real use cases, the price will likely decline as more supply unlocks.

✅ Our verdict: Pi is real, it's tradeable, and early miners have made money. Whether it becomes a long-term valuable asset depends on ecosystem development over the next 12-24 months. Don't hold Pi you can't afford to lose in value — but if you mined it for free, any return is a win.

The AI Connection

Interestingly, Pi Network has been exploring AI integration within its ecosystem — specifically using AI to improve KYC verification and fraud detection. Several Pi ecosystem apps are also experimenting with AI-powered features. Whether this gives Pi a meaningful edge remains to be seen, but it's worth watching.
